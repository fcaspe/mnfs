Frequently Asked Questions

What are the system requirements to run the plugin?

For the best experience, your system should meet these minimums:

macOS

  • Processor: Apple M1 chip or newer.
  • Operating System: macOS Sonoma (14.0) or newer.
  • RAM: 8 GB (16 GB or more is highly recommended).
  • Host: A 64-bit DAW supporting AU or VST3 formats (e.g., Logic Pro, Ableton Live).

Windows

  • Processor: Intel Core i7 (11th Gen) or equivalent AMD Ryzen processor (or newer).
  • Operating System: Windows 11 (64-bit). Windows 10 is supported but not guaranteed for future updates.
  • RAM: 8 GB (16 GB or more is highly recommended).
  • Host: A 64-bit DAW supporting VST3 format (e.g., Ableton Live, FL Studio, Cubase).
How do I install the plugin?

We provide installers for both macOS and Windows. The plugin is installed automatically at:

macOS Locations:

  • AU: /Library/Audio/Plug-Ins/Components/
  • VST3: /Library/Audio/Plug-Ins/VST3/

Windows Location:

  • VST3: C:\Program Files\Common Files\VST3\

Have your DAW rescan for new plugins. It should appear in your effects list.

How do I get the best performance and lowest latency?

Optimizing for low latency is a balancing act. Here are the key factors in order of importance:

  1. Adjust Your Buffer Size. This is the most critical setting. A lower buffer size reduces latency but demands more CPU power.
    • Start at 128 samples. This is a great balance of low latency and stability for most systems.
    • Try 64 samples for live tracking. This offers the lowest latency but may cause audio clicks or dropouts if your CPU is overloaded.
  2. (Windows Users) Use an ASIO Driver. For Windows, using an ASIO driver with an external audio interface is essential. It allows your DAW to bypass the standard Windows audio system, which dramatically improves performance.
  3. Stick to Your Project's Sample Rate. Don't increase your sample rate (e.g., from 48kHz to 96kHz) just to reduce latency. The benefit is minimal and it puts a very heavy load on your CPU.
